Decide for yourself

You arrived wanting to be told what to eat. You're leaving able to read any claim and answer that yourself. That was the whole point — and it's the last thing a course that respects you would hand over: the deciding.

Your toolkit — everything the first three modules gave you

M1

Read the panel

Per-100 g → serving → your day. Know the form (hearts vs powder) and the database (USDA / CNF).

M2

See the hidden fats

SCFA / MCFA / LCFA aren't one thing; "has omega-3" ≠ "has EPA/DHA."

M3

Size the claim

Energy density × amount. Completeness, not restriction — size up toward what's missing.

M1–3

Sort proved from claimed

Fact, hypothesis, or unsupported — always know which column a statement is in.

Now run the tools on a live claim

"Every adult should eat 100 g of hemp hearts a day."
1

What form? Hemp hearts — so USDA's 31.6 g protein / 553 kcal per 100 g applies (not the powder's 42–50%).

2

What does 100 g actually contain? ~553 kcal, 31.6 g complete protein, ~9 g ALA (plant ω-3, little/no EPA-DHA), 700 mg magnesium. All fact, from the panel.

3

Which part is a claim? "Every adult" and "100 g a day" — a universal daily prescription. That's not on any panel; it's an assertion about all people. Column: unsupported as universal.

4

So what do you do? Split it: the nutrition facts are real and useful; the one-size prescription isn't. You decide your amount, sized up toward what your own day is missing — with the facts in hand.

Your verdict, not ours: a food can be genuinely nutritious and "everyone must eat exactly this much" can be an overreach — both at once. You just held both without being sold either.
