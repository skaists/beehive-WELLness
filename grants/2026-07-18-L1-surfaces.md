## Independence limitation — read before citing this grant

Quoted verbatim from the auditor's own report, at its insistence:

> **I am a read-only agent spawned inside the author's own session. I am NOT a fully
> independent third party.** I did not author the artifacts, I hold no write tools, and I
> left the repository byte-unchanged — but I run in the same session, on the same host,
> under the same principal as the author. My process isolation is a matter of my own
> configuration, not of institutional separation. A reader who requires adversarial
> independence should treat this as a **structured self-check that the author cannot
> silently edit**, not as external attestation. **This grant should not be cited as
> third-party verification.**

Ladder §5.1 requires a grant to come from a read-only auditor and never from the author of
what it grades. This auditor satisfies both on the letter — read-only, and not the author.
It does **not** satisfy adversarial independence, which is a stronger bar than §5.1 sets and
a bar this grant does not claim to clear. **Upgrading to third-party attestation remains
open**, and this record should be superseded rather than amended if that happens.

## What undercuts this grant

Recorded because a grant that hides the defects in its own production is worth nothing.

**1 · The tree changed during its own grading, and the fault is the author's.** Commit
`750dfb2` was pushed by the author's session mid-audit, rewriting `surfaces/README.md`,
`SECURITY.md` and `scripts/phi-scan.sh`. The auditor did not write to the repository; it
detected the change by re-running `git status` before its controls.

Containment was **verified, not assumed**: `git diff --stat 3fccb98 HEAD -- 'surfaces/*.html'`
is empty, so all 8 graded artifacts are byte-identical across the new commit; the digest
table is unchanged; the D1 text is unchanged; and all 8 live copies were re-fetched with
`Cache-Control: no-cache` after the commit and still matched. The grant is unaffected.

The process defect stands regardless. **A tree that can change during its own grading is one
where a later reader cannot assume the audited state was frozen.** Future grants must be run
against a detached checkout or a tag. This is recorded against the author, who had written
earlier the same day that changing an artifact mid-audit makes the audit worthless, and then
did it.

**2 · No render was observed with egress actually blocked.** A1's rendering clause was
verified structurally — zero external references, zero runtime egress vectors, all assets
inline, 8/8 structurally complete documents. It follows *by construction* that these files
render identically with the network unplugged, since there is nothing to fetch. Sound by
construction, but an inference rather than a screenshot in an egress-blocked browser.

**3 · Served verification is point-in-time.** Digests matched at two moments today. The
disclosure itself concedes the operator can withdraw at any time. Nothing in A1 binds future
delivery; this certifies bytes observed now.

**4 · The mid-audit commit added an author claim the auditor did not check.** The new README
section asserts the founder reviewed the eight live served surfaces at their public URLs.
The auditor did not verify that a human opened anything — outside its scope and outside
L1's requirements. **Not load-bearing for this grant**, and a later reader should not mistake
it for something the auditor checked.

**5 · Digest provenance is trust-on-first-use.** Local == recorded == served is established.
That the recorded digests were committed *before* publication rather than back-filled to
match rests on the integrity of the git history, which was not audited commit by commit.
